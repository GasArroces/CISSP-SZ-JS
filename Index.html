1. Introducción y Visión General

Este capítulo, "Security Governance Through Principles and Policies," es fundamental porque aborda los elementos fundacionales de las soluciones de seguridad y sienta las bases para el Dominio 1.0: Security and Risk Management, que representa un 16% del examen.

Clave para el Examen CISSP

Es crucial comprender que la seguridad es una herramienta de gestión empresarial, no solo una preocupación de TI. Su propósito principal es apoyar los objetivos, la misión y las metas de la organización. Una confusión común en el examen es enfocarse demasiado en los detalles técnicos sin alinear las soluciones de seguridad con la estrategia de negocio.

2. Fundamentos

La seguridad es esencial: asegura que una organización pueda existir y operar a pesar de los intentos de robar datos o comprometer elementos físicos o lógicos.

Evaluación de la Seguridad

Risk Assessment: Identificación de activos, amenazas y vulnerabilidades para calcular el riesgo.

Vulnerability Assessment: Uso de herramientas automatizadas para localizar debilidades de seguridad conocidas.

Penetration Testing: Puesta a prueba de la infraestructura de seguridad por equipos de confianza para encontrar problemas antes que los adversarios.

Punto Clave de Examen: Distinguir claramente estos métodos de evaluación es clave. La confusión a menudo reside en usarlos indistintamente, cuando cada uno tiene un propósito y alcance diferente.

3. Conceptos y Principios

Estos son los pilares conceptuales sobre los que se construye toda la seguridad.

Security

CIA
  • Confidentiality: Prevenir la divulgación no autorizada de datos.
  • Integrity: Proteger los datos contra modificaciones no autorizadas.
  • Availability: Asegurar el acceso oportuno a los datos por usuarios autorizados.
AAA
  • Identification + Authentication: Declarar y verificar una identidad.
  • Authorization: Conceder permisos a una identidad verificada.
  • Auditing: Registrar las actividades de los sujetos.
  • Accounting: Hacer a los sujetos responsables de sus acciones.

Más Allá de la Triada: Relevancia para el CISSP

Authenticity

Es crucial porque valida el origen de la información. En transacciones complejas, no basta con asegurar que los datos no se han filtrado (Confidentiality) o alterado (Integrity); debemos tener la certeza de que provienen de quien dice ser. Esto previene ataques de suplantación (spoofing) y es la base de la confianza en las interacciones digitales.

Non-Repudiation

Proporciona una prueba irrefutable de que una acción ocurrió. Se construye sobre los servicios de AAA (especialmente Auditing y Accounting). Para obligaciones legales, financieras y contractuales, es fundamental poder demostrar que un usuario específico realizó una acción y no puede negarlo. Esto es clave para la rendición de cuentas (accountability) y la resolución de disputas, una preocupación central desde la perspectiva de negocio y riesgos del CISSP.

5. Due Diligence y Due Care

Conceptos legales y éticos fundamentales, definidos en el contexto de la seguridad.

Conceptos Clave

  • Due Care: Es la protección responsable de los activos.
  • Due Diligence: Es la capacidad de probar el Due Care.

Due Care

Protección responsable de los activos, basada y alineada con las metas y objetivos de la organización.

Esta definición alinea lo que la seguridad debería estar haciendo con lo que la organización necesita. Es lo que "Due Care" significa desde una perspectiva de seguridad.

Due Diligence

La capacidad de probar el Due Care a las partes interesadas (alta gerencia, reguladores, clientes, etc.).

Due Diligence es lo que se hace para probar el Due Care de forma regular a las partes interesadas de la organización.

Punto Clave de Examen: Una de las confusiones más comunes. La distinción entre planificar (diligence) y ejecutar (care) es crucial.

7. Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery

Asegurar que el "show must go on" para la compañía, diferenciando el enfoque estratégico del negocio del táctico de TI.

SHOW MUST GO ON

BCP - Business Continuity Planning
  • Enfoque: Operaciones del Negocio
  • Nivel: Estratégico (Largo Plazo)
  • Perspectiva: Alto nivel, relacionado con la gestión de riesgos del negocio.
DR - Disaster Recovery
  • Enfoque: Actividades de TI
  • Nivel: Táctico (Corto Plazo)
  • Perspectiva: Micro-nivel, enfocado en emergencias inmediatas de TI.

4 Pasos del Proceso de BCP

1. Project Scope & Planning

Análisis de la organización, selección del equipo, requerimientos.

2. Business Impact Analysis (BIA)

Identificar prioridades, riesgos, impacto y recursos.

3. Continuity Planning

Desarrollo de la estrategia y el plan de continuidad.

4. Approval & Implementation

Aprobación del plan, implementación y mantenimiento.

8. Org Roles and Responsabilities

Veamos cómo se aplican los roles y responsabilidades en el contexto de "Tío Rico Bank".

Propietarios / Controladores / Líderes Funcionales / Alta Gerencia

Accountable for:
  • Asegurar que los controles de seguridad apropiados, consistentes con la política de Tío Rico Bank, sean implementados para proteger los activos.
  • Determinar los niveles apropiados de sensibilidad o clasificación de los datos de los clientes.
  • Determinar los privilegios de acceso.

Profesionales de Seguridad de Sistemas de Información / Oficial de Seguridad de TI

Responsible for:
  • Diseño, implementación, gestión y revisión de las políticas, estándares, baselines, procedimientos y guías de seguridad de Tío Rico Bank.

Usuarios

Responsible for:
  • Adherencia a las políticas de seguridad.
  • Preservar la disponibilidad, integridad y confidencialidad de los activos al acceder a ellos y utilizarlos.

Oficial de Tecnología (IT)

Responsible for:
  • Desarrollar e implementar soluciones tecnológicas de seguridad.
  • Trabajar de cerca con los profesionales de seguridad para evaluar estrategias.
  • Colaborar con el equipo de Business Continuity Management (BCM) para asegurar la continuidad de operaciones.

Función de TI / Operador / Administrador / Administrador de Red

Responsible for:
  • Implementar y adherirse a las políticas de seguridad en Tío Rico Bank.
  • Gestionar, solucionar problemas y aplicar parches de hardware y software.
  • Administrar permisos de usuario y aplicaciones específicas.
  • Mantener las redes informáticas y configurar equipos de red.

Auditores de Sistemas de Información

Responsible for:
  • Proporcionar a la gerencia una garantía independiente de que los objetivos de seguridad se están cumpliendo.
  • Determinar si la política, los estándares y las guías son apropiados y efectivos para cumplir con los objetivos de seguridad de Tío Rico Bank.

9. Preguntas de Repaso

1. Una organización está desarrollando una nueva política de seguridad para el trabajo remoto. La dirección busca la máxima flexibilidad, mientras que el equipo de seguridad insiste en controles estrictos. Según los principios de gobierno de seguridad, ¿cuál es el enfoque más adecuado?

Respuesta Correcta: D.
La gobernanza de seguridad no se trata de imponer controles absolutos, sino de gestionar el riesgo de una manera que se alinee con los objetivos del negocio. Un enfoque basado en riesgos permite encontrar un equilibrio entre la habilitación del negocio (flexibilidad) y la protección de activos, involucrando a todas las partes interesadas para crear una política que sea tanto efectiva como práctica.

2. Una empresa descubre una brecha de datos que involucra PII de clientes en EE. UU. y la UE. ¿Cuál es el paso inicial más crítico antes de realizar una notificación generalizada?

Respuesta Correcta: B.
Las leyes de notificación de brechas (como GDPR en la UE y las leyes estatales en EE. UU.) tienen requisitos muy específicos sobre a quién notificar, cuándo y qué información incluir. Estos requisitos dependen directamente de la residencia de las personas afectadas y del tipo de datos comprometidos. Por lo tanto, determinar el alcance exacto es el primer paso legal y de cumplimiento esencial.

3. En un modelado de amenazas de una app bancaria, se identifica un riesgo de "doble gasto" de fondos. Según el modelo STRIDE, ¿qué tipo de amenaza es esta?

Respuesta Correcta: D.
Tampering se refiere a la modificación no autorizada de datos. Un ataque de "doble gasto" implica manipular el estado de una transacción o los datos del sistema para procesar un pago dos veces, lo cual es un ataque directo a la integridad de los datos y, por lo tanto, se clasifica como Tampering.

4. Una organización tiene un RPO de 4 horas y un RTO de 24 horas para un sistema crítico. ¿Qué estrategia de recuperación cumple mejor con el RPO?

Respuesta Correcta: C.
Un RPO de 4 horas significa que la organización no puede permitirse perder más de 4 horas de datos. El "remote journaling" o "mirroring" replica los datos a un sitio secundario de forma continua o casi continua, lo que garantiza una pérdida de datos mínima (a menudo de segundos o minutos), cumpliendo fácilmente con un RPO estricto. Las copias de seguridad diarias o el "vaulting" por hora podrían no cumplir con este requisito.

5. Un programa de concientización de seguridad no es efectivo (alta tasa de clics en phishing). ¿Cuál es el siguiente paso más efectivo para mejorarlo?

Respuesta Correcta: C.
El objetivo de la concientización es cambiar el comportamiento humano. Los métodos pasivos o punitivos a menudo son ineficaces. La "gamificación" (hacerlo divertido y competitivo) y los "security champions" (compañeros que promueven la seguridad) fomentan una cultura de seguridad positiva y un aprendizaje activo, lo que ha demostrado ser más efectivo para lograr un cambio de comportamiento duradero.

10. 20 Questions to Test

1. Gavin is creating a report for management on the results of his most recent risk assessment. In his report, he would like to identify the remaining level of risk to the organization after adopting security controls. What term best describes this current level of risk?

Answer: B.
Justification: Residual risk is the risk that remains after security controls have been implemented. Inherent risk is the amount of risk that exists before any controls are put in place.

2. Francine is a security specialist for an online service provider in the United States. She recently received a claim from a copyright holder that a user is storing information on her service that violates the third party's copyright. What law governs the actions that Francine must take?

Answer: A.
Justification: The Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) provides a safe harbor for online service providers who promptly remove copyrighted content upon receiving a claim of copyright infringement from the copyright holder. Francine's actions would be governed by the DMCA.

3. Renee is purchasing a new software product and is working with the vendor on the negotiation of a license agreement that will specify customized terms of use and a discounted price. What type of agreement would normally be used to document the results of this negotiation?

Answer: C.
Justification: An enterprise license agreement is used when an organization negotiates customized terms of use and pricing for a software product. The other options are general license types.

4. Henry recently assisted one of his co-workers in preparing for the CISSP® exam. During this process, Henry disclosed confidential information about the content of the exam, in violation of Canon IV of the Code of Ethics: “Advance and protect the profession.” Who may bring ethics charges against Henry for this violation?

Answer: A.
Justification: Anyone may bring ethics charges against an ISC2-certified professional, member, or candidate for a violation of the Code of Ethics.

5. Wanda is working with one of her organization's European Union business partners to facilitate the exchange of customer information. Wanda's organization is located in the United States. What would be the best method for Wanda to use to ensure GDPR compliance?

Answer: A.
Justification: Binding corporate rules (BCRs) are a common method used by organizations to ensure GDPR compliance when transferring personal data internationally within a multinational group of companies.

6. Chris is advising travelers from his organization who will be visiting many different countries overseas. He is concerned about compliance with export control laws. Which of the following technologies is most likely to trigger these regulations?

Answer: D.
Justification: Encryption software is the most likely technology on the list to trigger export control laws due to its potential dual-use nature (commercial and military applications).

7. Bobbi is investigating a security incident and discovers that an attacker began with a normal user account but managed to exploit a system vulnerability to provide that account with administrative rights. What type of attack took place under the STRIDE threat model?

Answer: D.
Justification: Elevation of privilege under the STRIDE threat model occurs when an attacker gains more privileges than they are authorized for.

8. You are completing your business continuity planning effort and have decided that you want to accept one of the risks. What should you do next?

Answer: D.
Justification: Whenever you choose to accept a risk, you should maintain detailed documentation of the risk acceptance process to satisfy auditors in the future.

9. Tony is developing a business continuity plan and is having difficulty prioritizing resources because of the difficulty of combining information about tangible and intangible assets. What would be the most effective risk assessment approach for him to use?

Answer: D.
Justification: Tony would see the best results by combining elements of quantitative and qualitative risk assessment. Quantitative risk assessment excels at analyzing financial risk, while qualitative risk assessment is a good tool for intangible risks. Combining the two techniques provides a well-rounded risk picture.

10. Vincent believes that a former employee took trade secret information from his firm and brought it with him to a competitor. He wants to pursue legal action. Under what law could he pursue charges?

Answer: D.
Justification: The Economic Espionage Act imposes fines and jail sentences on anyone found guilty of stealing trade secrets from a U.S. corporation. It gives true teeth to the intellectual property rights of trade secret owners.

11. Brenda's organization recently completed the acquisition of a competitor firm. Which one of the following tasks would be LEAST likely to be part of the organizational processes addressed during the acquisition?

Answer: C.
Justification: The protection of intellectual property is a greater concern during a divestiture, where a subsidiary is being spun off into a separate organization, than an acquisition, where one firm has purchased another.

12. Kelly believes that an employee engaged in the unauthorized use of computing resources for a side business. After consulting with management, she decides to launch an administrative investigation. What is the burden of proof that she must meet in this investigation?

Answer: D.
Justification: Unlike criminal or civil cases, administrative investigations are an internal matter, and there is no set standard of proof that Kelly must apply.

13. Laura has been asked to perform a security controls assessment (SCA). What type of organization is she most likely in?

Answer: C.
Justification: The term security controls assessment (SCA) is often used in government contexts, particularly within the NIST Risk Management Framework.

14. Carl is a federal agent investigating a computer crime case. He identified an attacker who engaged in illegal conduct and wants to pursue a case against that individual that will lead to imprisonment. What standard of proof must Carl meet?

Answer: C.
Justification: In a criminal case, such as the one Carl is pursuing, the prosecutor must meet the beyond a reasonable doubt standard of proof.

15. Renee is designing a long-term security plan for her organization and has a three- to five-year planning horizon. Her primary goal is to align the security function with the broader plans and objectives of the business. What type of plan is she developing?

Answer: D.
Justification: Strategic plans have a long-term planning horizon of up to five years in most cases. They are designed to strategically align the security function with the business' objectives.

16. Gina is working to protect a logo that her company will use for a new product they are launching. She has questions about the intellectual property protection process for this logo. What U.S. government agency would be best able to answer her questions?

Answer: A.
Justification: A trademark is the correct intellectual property protection mechanism for a logo. Therefore, Gina should contact the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO), which bears responsibility for the registration of trademarks.

17. Tom enables an application firewall provided by his cloud infrastructure as a service provider that is designed to block many types of application attacks. When viewed from a risk management perspective, what metric is Tom attempting to lower by implementing this countermeasure?

Answer: D.
Justification: By blocking application attacks, Tom is reducing the likelihood of a successful attack and, therefore, reducing the associated risk.

18. You are the CISO for a major hospital system and are preparing to sign a contract with a software-as-a-service (SaaS) email vendor. You want to perform a control assessment to ensure that its business continuity planning measures are reasonable. What type of audit might you request to meet this goal?

Answer: D.
Justification: A SOC 2 report focuses on controls related to security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy, making it suitable for assessing a SaaS vendor's business continuity planning measures.

19. Gary is analyzing a security incident and, during his investigation, encounters a user who denies having having performed an action that Gary believes he did perform. What type of threat has taken place under the STRIDE model?

Answer: A.
Justification: Repudiation occurs when a user denies having performed an action, and the system lacks sufficient evidence to prove otherwise. This is a common concern in incident investigations.

20. Finally, there are historical records stored on the server that are extremely important to the business and should never be modified. You would like to add an integrity control that allows you to verify on a periodic basis that the files were not modified. What control can you add?

Answer: A.
Justification: Hashing is a strong integrity control. By regularly computing a hash of the files and comparing it to a baseline, any unauthorized modifications would be detected.

11. 30 Questions to Test DOM2

1. COBIT (Control Objectives for Information and Related Technology) is a framework for the management and governance of information technology (IT). What data management role is most likely to select and apply COBIT to balance the need for security controls with business requirements?

Answer: A.
Justification: Business owners have to balance the need to provide value with regulatory, security, and other requirements. This makes the adoption of a common framework like COBIT attractive.

2. As a DBA, Amy's data role in her organization includes technical implementations of the data policies and standards, as well as managing the data structures that the data is stored in. What data role best fits what Amy does?

Answer: A.
Justification: Amy is a data custodian and is responsible for the technical environment, including things like database structures and the technical implementations of data policies.

3. Which one of the following data roles bears ultimate organizational responsibility for data?

Answer: C.
Justification: The data owner has ultimate responsibility for the data of an organization and is typically the CEO, president, or another senior-level employee.

4. Your organization leverages memory-resident databases to improve system performance... What term best describes data that is resident in system memory?

Answer: C.
Justification: Data that is resident in system memory is considered data in use. Data can be at rest (on a disk), in use (in memory), or in transit (across a network).

5. What technique could you use to mark your trade secret information in case it was released or stolen and you need to identify it?

Answer: C.
Justification: Watermarks are a technique that can be used to embed an identifier into digital content, which would allow the trade secret information to be identified if it were released or stolen.

6. What type of encryption is best suited for use on the file servers for the proprietary data, and how might you secure the data when it is in motion?

Answer: B.
Justification: AES is a strong modern symmetric encryption algorithm that is appropriate for encrypting data at rest. TLS is frequently used to secure data when it is in transit.

7. Fred wants to classify his organization's data using common labels: private, sensitive, public, and proprietary. Which of the following should he apply to his highest classification level based on common industry practices?

Answer: D.
Justification: Common practice makes proprietary or confidential data the most sensitive data. The other options represent lower classification levels.

8. If you are selecting a security standard for a Windows 11 system that processes credit cards, what security standard is your best choice?

Answer: C.
Justification: The Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) provides the set of requirements for credit card processing systems.

9. How should you determine which controls from the CIS baseline should be applied to a given system or software package?

Answer: B.
Justification: The controls implemented from a security baseline should match the data classification of the data used or stored on the system.

10. A company in the EU collects customer data and sends it to a third party for analysis. What term best describes the third-party analysis company?

Answer: D.
Justification: The third-party company is a data processor—they process data on behalf of the company, which is a data controller. The data is collected about data subjects.

11. What concern should be raised about reusing systems from a Top Secret project for a future Secret project after purging?

Answer: B.
Justification: Downgrading systems is rare due to difficulty ensuring complete sanitization. The cost of wiping/destroying media can exceed the cost of new equipment.

12. A healthcare company's data owner believes exposure of certain non-medical patient data could cause damage (but not exceptional damage). How should this data be classified?

Answer: C.
Justification: Private data is internal business data that shouldn't be exposed but doesn't meet the "severe damage" threshold for confidential.

13. What technology could help prevent confidential data from being emailed out of the organization?

Answer: A.
Justification: Data Loss Prevention (DLP) systems are designed to identify and prevent the unauthorized transmission of sensitive data.

14. Using the U.S. government's data classification system, how should a system containing Secret, Confidential, and Top Secret data be classified?

Answer: A.
Justification: When data is stored in a mixed classification environment, it is typically classified based on the highest classification of data included.

15. As a DBA, Amy's data role includes technical implementations of data policies. What data role best fits what Amy does?

Answer: A.
Justification: Amy is a data custodian and is responsible for the technical environment, including things like database structures and the technical implementations of data policies.

16. Chris is a data owner. Which steps in the classification process is he most likely responsible for: 1. Set criteria, 2. Establish owners, 3. Classify data, 4. Select controls, 5. Select baselines?

Answer: A.
Justification: It is most likely that Chris would be responsible for classifying the data he owns (3), as well as helping or advising system owners on security requirements and control selection (4, 5).

17. Chris manages a team of system administrators who carry out steps 6, 7, and 8 (scoping, applying, enforcing controls). What data role are they fulfilling?

Answer: B.
Justification: System administrators act as data administrators who grant access and also as custodians tasked with the day-to-day application of security controls.

18. If Chris's company in the EU handles data for a third party, what role is his company operating in?

Answer: C.
Justification: Third-party organizations that process personal data on behalf of a data controller are known as data processors.

19. What data role does a system that is used to process data have?

Answer: C.
Justification: Systems used to process data are data processors.

20. What data roles own step 1 (categorization), are delegated step 2 (control selection), and are responsible for step 3 (implementation) in the NIST framework?

Answer: A.
Justification: The data owner is responsible for categorizing information systems and delegates the selection of controls to system owners, while custodians implement the controls.

21. Which of the following data roles is typically found as a third-party contracting relationship rather than inside a company?

Answer: D.
Justification: Data processors are typically third parties hired for specific tasks, while data owners, data controllers, and data custodians are typically internal roles within a company.

22. Why is data location a significant concern for data owners, controllers, and custodians?

Answer: B.
Justification: Data location, particularly at rest, may drive compliance requirements based on local or national laws. This concern drives the majority of data location concerns.

23. Angela wants to implement data security controls from NIST 800-53. She works to determine which controls are appropriate for her organization's needs. What term best describes this action?

Answer: A.
Justification: Scoping is the process of determining which controls are appropriate to an organization, environment, or implementation.

24. What type of tool is best suited to identifying data like Social Security numbers, credit card numbers, and similar well-understood data formats?

Answer: B.
Justification: A sensitive data scanning tool is designed to automatically identify known data patterns such as Social Security or credit card numbers.

25. What does labeling data allow a DLP system to do?

Answer: A.
Justification: Data loss prevention (DLP) systems can use labels on data to determine the appropriate controls to apply to the data.

26. Megan wants to prepare media for reuse in an environment operating at the same sensitivity level. Which of the following is the best option?

Answer: A.
Justification: Clearing is a sanitization method that prepares media for reuse in an environment operating at the same sensitivity level.

27. Information maintained about an individual that can be used to distinguish or trace their identity is known as what type of information?

Answer: A.
Justification: Information maintained about an individual that can be used to distinguish or trace their identity is known as personally identifiable information (PII).
